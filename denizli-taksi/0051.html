20 Issues I Want I Might Inform My 20-12 months-Previous Self

  • Post author By admin
  • Post date March 20, 2020
  • No Comments on 20 Issues I Want I Might Inform My 20-12 months-Previous Self

IfI met with my 20-year-old self, I imagine I’d not so much sit down for a quick chat as have a serious discussion to guide her through the coming years. More importantly, I’d have to write down my advice so she wouldn’t forget.

I think it would look something like this:

1. There are two things you can know for sure: what is happening right now, and what you plan to do in the immediate future.

Absolutely everything else is speculation, projection, or the fodder of your imagination. You are not psychic. Your feelings are not all real. Most of the things you worry about are fearful delusions. You must differentiate reality from imagination for the sake of your sanity.

2. Intent is a self-fulfilling prophecy.

What you intend to do with your life is what will become of your life. No matter the setbacks, no matter how you feel, your intention—followed by action—will always dominate your outcomes.

Instead of trying to predict what will happen, plan for what will happen. One involves being anxious about the 10% you can’t control, and the other involves capitalizing on the 90% that you can.

3. You will start to crave what you repeatedly do.

Nothing will feel comfortable at first, because you aren’t used to it. What you condition yourself to is what you will want to do automatically. Choose wisely.

4. Answer your emails.

Inbox zero, baby. Inbox zero.

5. When something works, you will know because it… works.

You don’t have to wonder whether or not something is meant for you, because if it is, it will be. That’s all. When you’re doing the right work, it will come easily, and it will be well-received. When you meet the right person, you will just be together, no questions asked. What’s meant for you is what’s happening. It’s not as complicated as you might think.

6. Sometimes, you will have heavy, strong feelings and intense fears and there will be no way to rationalize your way back to inner calm.

Nobody tells you this, but sometimes you are going to feel like shit—there’s nothing to do but feel like shit until you don’t feel like shit anymore.

You won’t feel this way forever.

But you mightif you do one of two things: suppress it or overanalyze it.

Entertaining intense fears only makes them seem more real. Suppressing them keeps them stuck within you. There will be nothing to do but allow them to pass. It’s the meaning you ascribe to your feelings that’s tripping you up.

7. Overthinking feelings does not release them—it keeps them stuck.

Feelings are not in your head, they are in your body.

You need to stretch, shake, cry, do yoga, walk, talk to a friend, talk to a therapist. Junk journal, write down everything you feel and rip it up and throw it out. Clean your space, donate your old things. All of this is releasing.

Sitting around and thinking through the problem one more time is not letting it go — it’s keeping it stuck exactly where it is because you’re maintaining the energy, and attention, you’ve already spent on it.

8. This phrase will change your life: “What is the opposite of this experience?”

If you can’t figure out what you want, start with what you don’t want. What’s the opposite of what makes you uncomfortable? What’s the opposite of what you’re dissatisfied with? Whenever you’re unhappy, please ask yourself: “What is the opposite of this experience?” That’s what you want to create.

9. Part of having relationships is making sacrifices to show up, be there, and spend time with the people you love.

However, you cannot do this all the time, for everyone. Therefore, you need to know who, and what, you value.

10. Relationships are built on sharing time and doing things together.

Not promises, not problems. Not a 20-point checklist about why someone is so perfect for you. Love is a verb. Life is too. Show up for it.

11. If ever you do not know what to do, do nothing.

Just for one more hour, day, or night. If you don’t know how you’re going to get through (whatever) just wait one night. One day at a time makes a life.

12. If a problem goes away when you stop thinking about it, it’s just a fear. If it gets worse when you stop thinking about it, it’s actually a problem.

This will help you decide what actually requires your attention. To that end…

13. Problems beget actions.

If there is no action to be taken, you don’t have a problem.

14. You are not supposed to look the way you did in high school.

Nobody does. Nobody is supposedto.

15. Change how you do one tiny thing each day, and the effect will compound naturally.

Seriously, if you want to change anything about your life, just change the way you do one thing daily. You will see how much your little habits are massively impacting your life. Most of your outcomes are the result of behaviors you aren’t even conscious of.

16. Honor your discomfort.

Every time you find yourself consistently uncomfortable—especially regarding one particular aspect of your life—something deep within you is telling you that you are capable of more, deserving of more, and destined for more. Let it show you where you need to grow.

17. Simplicity and meaning are far more motivating long-term than extravagance and status.

Make your goals simple and fill each day with meaningful purpose. You will blossom naturally, whereas, if you start to pursue the opposite, you will slowly shut down.

18. Ambition is not exclusive to materiality.

You can, and should, be highly ambitious about living a peaceful, purposeful life. You should be highly ambitious about being healthy, or being happy, or having a great group of friends. These, too, are worthwhile pursuits that will impact you even more than making a lot of money.

19. Get to the point where money doesn’t control you.

That’s the goal of financial freedom: to be able to say “no” when you want to.

20. You are responsible for your life.

You are responsible for your outcomes. You are responsible for your happiness. You are responsible for your success. You are not inherently more or less capable than anyone else. You are not falling behind. Every single day, ask yourself what you can do to move your life forward. The steps will add up. You will arrive.

Don’t worry so much — but don’t slow down, either.
